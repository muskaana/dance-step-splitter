How it works

  1. 1

    Add a video

    Choose 💃 Dance or 🎤 Sing, then paste a video URL (YouTube, Instagram Reel, or TikTok) or upload a file. Dance uses body-motion detection to find moves; Sing uses audio silences to find verses/choruses. For best quality, download the source yourself and use Upload.

  2. 2

    Crop & process

    Skip the intro/outro: scrub the player, hit Use playhead → Start / End, then click Process Video. You can also pick multiple files to combine into one routine before processing.

  3. 3

    Edit segments (and lyrics)

    Auto-detected segments appear below the video. Click Edit to tweak start/end times, split at the playhead, add/delete, or rename. Editing a boundary auto-links the adjacent segment so there are no gaps. For singing entries, each segment also has a lyrics text box — what you type shows up live under the segments while playing.

  4. 4

    Pick your practice settings

    Below the video: change Speed (slo-mo without chipmunk pitch), set a Break between loops, toggle Mirror Mode for dance, switch On Audio / On Counts for a spoken 1–8, or flip on Memory Mode to black out the video and practice from audio alone. With Memory Mode on, the Webcam Mirror toggle replaces the black screen with your own mirrored camera feed.

  5. 5

    Practice

    Click segment buttons to loop them (or combine several to drill a run). Use the dropdown overlay in the top-right of the video to jump between segments while watching, and ↻ to restart the current loop. For a guided session, hit Start Workshop — it drills segment 1, drills segment 2, combines 1+2, drills 3, combines 1+2+3, and so on. Reps within a drill repeat instantly; the Break setting pauses between different drills.

Library & sharing

  • Every processed video saves to your Library (top of the page). Open one to pick up where you left off.
  • Rename a video by clicking the ✎ next to its title in the library.
  • Share a video by username (direct share) or by link (anyone who signs in via the link gets access). View-only locks editing; edit-mode lets collaborators tweak the same segments — changes sync within ~15 s.
  • Combine lets you pick multiple library entries and splice them into one routine, preserving each segment's labels and lyrics.
